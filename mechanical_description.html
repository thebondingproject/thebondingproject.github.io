Mechanical description

Introduction

The original frame

As it was reported in an image in the project overview, the original bonding machine is essentially a bulky 4-axis robot. It's composed of two main elements:

  • The heated bed: it allows for horizontal movement of along the X and Y axes;
  • The machine head: it's mounted at a fixed height, but its internals can move vertically along Z and can also rotate along θ.

Rebuild challenges

We needed build a frame to allow movement along the four axis with micrometer precision. This immediately raises three problems:

  • How can we achieve micrometer precision while keeping costs sufficiently down?
  • How do we stop the bed from smashing accidentally against the metal frame? How do we prevent over-rotation of the machine head?
  • How do we set the zeros of X, Y, Z and θ reliably?

The new frame

Overview

The current state of the machine frame is depicted in the images below. The frame which supports the machine head was built from scratch, together with the motor mounts, the travel limiting sensors and the encoders’ mounts. The latter are discussed in detail in the motor control page. In the following section we will show the X, Y, Z, θ axes in greater detail, which use three different methods to achieve motion.

X, Y mechanism and limit switches

The image cluster below shows the X and Y axis mechanisms in greater detail.

  • Top-left: the heated bed is moved through a screw mechanism which translates the rotational movement of the servo motors into linear motion. The horizontal screw has a pitch of 0.75mm, meaning that for each revolution of the screw the heated bed moves horizontally by 0.75mm. This allows for great horizontal precison, which will enable us to achieve the desidered micrometer precision that we need to perform the bonds;
  • Top-right: a detail of the X motor;
  • Bottom-left: an animation of the screw mechanism to convert the rotational motion of the motor into linear horizontal motion;
  • Bottom-right: detail of the mechanical limit switch of the X axis. These switch are normally open; when they get hit by the heated bed, they form a short which is dected by the machine's MCU, which in turn proceeds to immediately halt the motors.

Z, θ mechanism and limit switches

The image cluster below shows the Z and θ axis mechanisms in greater detail.

  • Top-left: the Z motor moves the machine head up and down using a slider-crank mechanism, while the θ rotates the operating head with a belt drive mechanism;
  • Top-right: a detail of the Z and θ mechanisms. The belt drive of θ was not visibile in the top-left image;
  • Bottom-left: an outline of a slider-crank mechanism;
  • Bottom-right: highlight of the optical limit switches of the Z movement. The θ axis has optical limit switches as well, but they cannot be esily seen fron the outside and therefore they haven't been shown here. These switches are normally closed. When the light path gets interrupted by excessive travel or due to the gold bonding wire snapping accidentally, the machine gets immedately halted by the MCU.
