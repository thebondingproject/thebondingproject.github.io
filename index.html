The Bonding Project

Building a wedge-bond machine (almost) from scratch

The Bonding Project is a lab project led by Electronic Engineering students at the University of Udine. The aim is to create a cost-effective bonding machine by repurposing the core of an old industrial bonding machine and designing the necessary additional hardware to make it fully functional.

  • Project Overview

The Bonding Project in Pills

Why?

We want to perform repairs on broken wire bonds in RF chips of vintage RF lab equipment. The fascination of the challenge and the satisfaction of performing precision repairs is our driving force.

IC bonding on the cheap

Wire bonding machines are expensive. Repurposing the decommissioned core of an old bonding machine is significantly cheaper than buying a new one.

Pathfinding

We have very little documentation about the decommissioned part we'll be using. We have no information about the additional hardware that's needed to make it work. We'll need fill the gaps ourselves.

Extreme mechanical precision

The IC pads for wire bonding are 50 microns wide, half the average width of a human hair. Four-axis motor control must be extraordinarily fine and precise.

Teamwork and cooperation

Complex projects require the expertise of multiple people. This requires teamwork, coordination and effective time management.

A multi-disciplinary feat

This project will need multi-disciplinary knowledge: power electronics, PCB design and assembly, microcontroller programming, control theory, optics, intrumentation and sensors...
