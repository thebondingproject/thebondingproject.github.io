Bonding the proper way

Introduction

The first bonding test we performed (see here) had some limitations that we needed to address:

  • We used a makeshift interface made with the STM32CubeMonitor to control the machine;
  • The Z motor was not yet calibrated properly (see the motor calibration procedure here). This forced us to control the Z axis by manually by moving the slider-crank mechanism by hand;
  • The solenoids were operated manually;
  • The ultrasonic driver was activated manually.
In this section we describe how we implemented the proper semi-automatic bonding procedure, i.e.:
  • Pressing a physical button initiates the head descent;
  • The ultrasonic driver is activated automatically, making the first bond;
  • The machine enters in jog mode, allowing the user to move to the second bond location;
  • The machine lowers its head again and activates the ultrasonic driver automatically, making the second bond;\
  • The head raises back to its resting position.

Implementing the bonding procedure

By using the documentation reported above we designed the following bonding procedure:

  • Wait for the button press to start the bonding procedure;
  • When the button is pressed, lower the head to the first search height (i.e., the head hangs a few mm above the substrate);
  • When the button is pressed again, lower the head until the substrate is touched. Then, lower the head a few microns more to exert a little pressure on the substrate;
  • Perform the bond by activating the ultrasonic driver for a short time interval;
  • Open the clamp by turning on its solenoid;
  • Raise the head to the bond height position (this parameter sets the height of the loop of wire constituting the bond);
  • Let the user move the XY platter freely;
  • When the button is pressed, lower the head to the second search height (which is in general different from the first search height);
  • When the button is pressed again, lower the head until the substrate is touched. Then, lower the head a few microns more to exert a little pressure on the substrate;
